## How to build your Stakeholder Map (inside + outside)

You can present this as a concrete mini-process the reader can run:

### Identify stakeholders

Brainstorm all people/groups who are affected, have influence, or have a strong interest in your product or team.

Split them into “Inside” (internal) and “Outside” (external).

### Analyze power and interest

For each stakeholder, rate their power (influence on priorities/resources) and interest (how much they care) on a simple 1–10 scale or low/medium/high.

Use this to decide how often and how deeply you need to engage each one.

### Map them visually

Put them on a simple 2x2 power–interest grid: high power/high interest, high power/low interest, etc.

This becomes your “WAN topology”: who needs high-bandwidth communication vs. low-bandwidth updates.

### Define expectations (two-way contract)

For each important stakeholder, write down:

What they expect from you: outcomes, visibility, cadence, format (e.g., “monthly roadmap review”).

What you expect from them: decisions, inputs, constraints, timely feedback.

This mirrors RACI-style clarity but at the relationship level, not per-task.

Review and update regularly

Revisit the map when priorities shift, org structure changes, or new projects start.

Move stakeholders on the map as their involvement and influence change.

For each stakeholder:

### Why they care about your team.

### What they expect (outcomes, responsiveness, quality).

### How and how often you communicate (channel + cadence).

## Working agreements

### One‑page “how we work together” with your main internal stakeholders.

### Clarify response times, change process, escalation paths.

Key idea: “Don’t guess expectations. Write them down together and revisit after big changes.”
